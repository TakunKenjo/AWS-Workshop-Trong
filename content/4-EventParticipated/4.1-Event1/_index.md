# Event Report: "Cloud Architect" Technology Game Show

### Purpose of the Event

- To create a positive learning environment for knowledge sharing, networking, and collaboration among members and intern teams.
- To reinforce and systematize in-depth knowledge of Cloud Computing and the principles of Cloud Architecture.
- To develop teamwork skills and strategic thinking under time pressure while providing opportunities for the audience to interact and learn from real-world scenarios.

### Key Highlights

- **Head-to-Head Competition Format:** The event featured 8 competing teams, each consisting of 5 members. Teams competed directly against each other in elimination rounds, with the two best-performing teams advancing to the Grand Final.

- **Question Set Structure:** Each match consisted of a set of 10 core questions related to Cloud Computing and Cloud Architecture. The questions were arranged in increasing order of difficulty, progressing from easy to challenging.

- **Winning Mechanism:** The team with the higher total score after the 10 questions advanced to the next round. In the event of a tie, an additional tie-breaker question (Question 11) was activated, and the team that answered both faster and more accurately was declared the winner.

### Rules & Skill Usage Mechanism (Strategy)

The competition followed a strict scoring system: correct answers earned points, while incorrect answers resulted in point deductions. Therefore, the strategic use of the two support skills (each usable only once) played a crucial role.

- **"Minimum Risk" Skill:** This skill was suitable for questions where the team was uncertain about the answer. If the answer was incorrect, no points were deducted. If the answer was correct, the team received half of the question's original score.

- **"Star of Hope" Skill:** This skill was ideal for questions in which the team was highly confident. A correct answer doubled the question's score, while an incorrect answer resulted in double the point deduction.

### Lessons Learned (From the Perspective of an Audience Member)

- **Technical Knowledge:** Although I attended as an audience member, following the entire competition—from the easiest to the most difficult questions—helped me systematically review my knowledge. The organizers' explanations and the teams' problem-solving approaches provided valuable real-world insights into cloud architecture design.

- **Observing and Evaluating Strategies:** As an observer, I learned how teams analyzed risks when deciding whether to use the "Star of Hope" or "Minimum Risk" support skills. Watching from the audience allowed me to clearly see how timely decisions—or mistakes—could significantly influence the outcome of a match.

- **Teamwork Lessons:** I learned effective collaboration, role distribution, and time management by observing the competing teams. Even when facing difficult questions, some teams remained calm, discussed the problem thoroughly, and reached a consensus before answering.

### Applications to Work and Study

- **Enhancing Professional Knowledge:** I took notes on challenging topics and real-world cloud scenarios introduced during the game show so that I could further study them and better prepare for future large-scale projects.

- **Communication and Team Collaboration:** By analyzing the strengths and weaknesses of each team's collaboration style, I gained practical insights that I can apply to improve my communication and teamwork skills in future academic group projects.

### Real Experience at the Event

Participating in the **"Cloud Architect"** program as an audience member was a completely new, exciting, and rewarding experience. Some of my most memorable impressions include:

- **High-Quality Technical Content:** The questions prepared by the organizers were quite challenging and required analytical thinking rather than simple theoretical knowledge. While watching the competition, I also challenged myself by timing my responses and attempting to answer the questions independently, which continuously stimulated my critical thinking.

- **Relaxed and Energetic Atmosphere:** Although the competition itself was highly competitive, the overall atmosphere remained enjoyable and energetic. One unique aspect was that the Master of Ceremonies (MC) changed throughout the event, from 9:00 AM until 12:30 PM, keeping the audience engaged and maintaining a lively environment.

- **Excellent Audience Interaction:** The organizers did not overlook the audience. The MC frequently interacted with attendees by asking questions, encouraging participation, and giving away prizes. This allowed me not only to watch the competition but also to actively participate, immerse myself in the event, and learn together with everyone else.
